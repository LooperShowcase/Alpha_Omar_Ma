Ferrari Enzo

Built in 2002 using pure Formula 1 technology, the Enzo was limited to exactly 399 units. It remains an irreplaceable, aggressive milestone in hypercar history.

F1 Technology for the Street

The Enzo utilized a carbon-fiber composite chassis shell, an active aerodynamic rear wing, and advanced carbon-ceramic brake systems. Its front nose design was sculpted to explicitly mirror the look of a real Schumacher-era Formula 1 racing machine.

V12 Powertrain Performance

Mounted directly behind the driver sits a massive 6.0L naturally aspirated V12 engine pushing out 651 horsepower. It flies from 0 to 100 km/h in an explosive 3.1 seconds and clears a jaw-dropping maximum top speed of 350 km/h.

Enzo Highlights

  1. 6.0L Tipo F140 B V12 Engine
  2. Carbon Fiber Monocoque Chassis
  3. Only 399 Units Ever Produced
