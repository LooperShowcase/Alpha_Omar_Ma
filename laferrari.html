LaFerrari

The Ferrari LaFerrari is a limited-production hybrid hypercar that serves as the definitive embodiment of the Italian automaker’s design, Formula 1 racing pedigree, and technological capabilities. Introduced in 2013 as the highly anticipated successor to the Ferrari Enzo, it represents Ferrari’s first-ever production hybrid vehicle.

HY-KERS Hybrid System

LaFerrari blends a screaming 6.3-liter naturally aspirated V12 engine with a powerful electric motor derived from Ferrari's Formula 1 KERS setup. Together, this combination generates a massive total of 950 horsepower, allowing instantaneous torque response out of tight corners.

Extreme Performance

Constructed entirely around a hand-laminated carbon fiber monocoque chassis shell, the hypercar delivers blistering pace. It charges from 0 to 100 km/h in less than 2.6 seconds, passing 300 km/h in a mere 15 seconds, before maxing out at an incredible top speed exceeding 350 km/h.

LaFerrari Highlights

  1. 6.3L V12 + Electric Motor Architecture
  2. 950 Total Combined Horsepower
  3. Limited Production of Just 499 Coupes
